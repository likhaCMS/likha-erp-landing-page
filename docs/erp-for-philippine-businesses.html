1. Centralized, Reliable Database
Your ERP should store all business data in **one unified system**, ensuring:

- No duplicate entries
- No conflicting versions
- No manual consolidation
- No ‚Äúlost‚Äù transactions

A strong database foundation is the heart of any ERP.

2. Real-Time Visibility Across Departments & Branches

Your ERP must allow you to see:

- Sales
- Inventory levels
- Purchases
- Expenses
- Approvals
- Pending tasks
- Branch performance

All in real time.

This is essential for decision-making, especially for:

- Multi-branch SMEs
- Distribution and wholesale
- Manufacturing
- Service companies
- Government procurement units
- Schools with multiple campuses

3. Hybrid Cloud with Offline Capabilities

In the Philippines, internet reliability varies by location.
An ERP should not stop operations just because the internet goes down.

A hybrid ERP allows:

- Continuous operations offline
- Automatic syncing once online
- Zero downtime
- Field operations even without signal
- LGU and barangay offices to operate despite connectivity issues

This is one of the biggest competitive advantages of Likha ERP.

4. Automated Workflows & Approvals

Businesses should replace manual paper-based processes with:

- Digital approval workflows
- Automated routing
- Notifications
- Audit trails
- Logs for transparency

This benefits SMEs, corporations, and especially **government units** where compliance and traceability are critical.

5. Flexible Modules for Any Industry

Look for an ERP that can support **multiple sectors**, including:

- Retail
- Wholesale & distribution
- Manufacturing
- Services
- Hospitality
- Schools
- Cooperatives
- Construction
- LGUs & government agencies

Essential modules include:

- Inventory
- Sales
- Purchasing
- HR & payroll
- Asset tracking
- Approvals
- Document management
- Project or job order management
- Finance and audit trails

6. Integrations With Other Systems

Your ERP should connect to:

- POS systems
- Marketplaces (Shopee, Lazada)
- Logistics providers
- Philippine banks
- Payment systems (GCash, Maya)
- Government systems (PhilGEPS, DICT APIs, eGov)
- Accounting tools
- Business intelligence dashboards

Integration ensures consistent data and eliminates manual encoding.

7. Scalability and Future-Proofing

Your ERP should grow with your business:

- Add branches
- Add more modules
- Add more users
- Expand workflows
- Support new integrations

A rigid system will limit your growth.
