🎯 Workshop Objectives

Understand (The Mental Model)

Break the misconception that you need to be a programmer to design a system. Process mapping and system building are leadership skills.

Experience (The Hands-On Build)

Provide attendees with a secure Likha ERP sandbox environment to literally build a working prototype of an operational process during the workshop.

Govern (Project Oversight)

Equip leaders with a 6-step project management framework to safely scale their digital initiatives across their entire organization.

Add Value

Leave attendees with a working digital prototype of their own process, culminating in the offer for a free "Step 1: Discovery Site Visit" to finish mapping the rest of their operations.

Workshop Agenda & Content Outline

Part 1: Executive Visibility & Process Mapping (The Diagnosis)

Focus: Identifying a bottleneck and mapping the logic before touching any software.

The Myth of the "IT Requirement": If you understand how a piece of paper moves from desk to desk in your office, you have all the skills required to build your digital system today.

The "Paper to Pixel" Fallacy: Why digitizing a bad process just gives you a faster bad process. The strategic importance of optimizing the workflow on paper first.

Interactive Activity (The Napkin Map): Attendees select one simple, frustrating process from their organization (e.g., a Purchase Request, a Leave Form, or an Inventory Requisition). They sketch the 3-step lifecycle of this transaction on a provided worksheet.

Part 2: The Hands-On Lab (Building Your System in Likha ERP)

Focus: Attendees open their laptops, log into a provided Likha ERP sandbox, and translate their "Napkin Map" into a live, working system using the 4 Building Blocks.

Block 1: Forms, Records & Custom Fields (Data Standardization)

The Action: Attendees use the drag-and-drop builder to create their digital form (e.g., adding text fields for "Item Name," dropdowns for "Department," and number fields for "Quantity").

The Realization: They see how easy it is to mandate exactly what data their staff must submit.

Block 2: Roles & Permissions (Governance & Security)

The Action: Attendees create two roles: "Requestor" and "Approver." They set the permissions so the Requestor can only submit, and the Approver can edit and approve.

The Realization: They physically build the digital chain-of-approval and understand how data is kept secure.

Block 3: Automation Flows (The Efficiency Engine)

The Action: Attendees set up a simple rule: If Status changes to 'Approved', then automatically deduct the 'Quantity' from Inventory OR send a notification email.

The Realization: The magic moment. They realize they just eliminated a manual administrative task without writing a single line of code.

Block 4: Dashboard Panels (The Leadership Dashboard)

The Action: Attendees generate a real-time pie chart showing "Pending vs. Approved Requests."

The Realization: They see how data naturally flows into an executive overview, eliminating the need to wait for weekly manual reports.

Part 3: Project Governance (Leading the Transformation)

Focus: Now that they know how easy it is to build one process, how do they scale this across an entire enterprise or agency?

Introduce the 6-Step Path to Successful System Implementation to maintain control over large-scale rollouts:

Step 1: Discovery (The Ground-Truth Audit): Mapping the actual, on-the-ground workflow.

Step 2: Blueprint (The Architecture Review): Planning the Forms, Roles, and Automations.

Step 3: Proposal (Resource Allocation): Securing transparent pricing based on exact blocks needed.

Step 4: Construction (The Build Phase): Expanding what they just did in the sandbox to the entire operation.

Step 5: Test and Train (The Validation): Ensuring frontline staff can actually use the system.

Step 6: Turnover & Support (Operational Readiness): Transitioning to a permanent, owned system.
